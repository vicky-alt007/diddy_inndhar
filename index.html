Diddy Inndhar (born 9 June 1969) is an Indian mechanical engineering student, gym-goer and self-proclaimed future party legend from Chennai. He is best known (mostly by himself) for his highly unusual life story: allegedly being discovered in Germany as a child with supposed distant ties to the Hitler family, following Nazi ideology for a few years as a small boy, dramatically converting to loving Diddy, hip-hop, democracy and Black culture, moving to India at age ~10, suffering intense social rejection, renaming himself after Diddy for confidence, and spending more than 20 years as an undergraduate at Velammal Engineering College while dreaming of hosting legendary parties that will finally get him friends.

Early life

According to the most entertaining version of events told by Inndhar himself, he was originally named Heinrich "Heinz" Müller and spent his first ~10 years in Germany. As a very young child he supposedly absorbed strong Nazi ideology — goose-stepping in kindergarten, drawing swastikas on lunch boxes, etc.

Everything changed in a legendary (and completely made-up) moment when he heard Diddy (or at least very loud hip-hop beats). According to Inndhar this single musical experience destroyed his entire worldview in seconds. He immediately started loving Black culture, democracy, equality, baggy clothes, shiny chains and basslines. He wrote fan letters to Diddy using crayons and tried to start underground Diddy fan clubs in Bavaria.

After being heavily bullied for this sudden personality 180°, he allegedly fled to India around age 10, arrived in Chennai, changed his name to Diddy Inndhar, ate a lot of idli and has been trying to become a legendary party host ever since.

Education

In 2004 Inndhar enrolled in the Mechanical Engineering program at Velammal Engineering College, Chennai.

As of March 2026 he is still studying there — making it one of the longest ongoing undergraduate degrees in Indian academic folklore. He is legendary among juniors for:

  • submitting assignments months late with party-themed cover pages
  • asking for grace marks "because friendship is also engineering"
  • proposing final-year projects titled "Aerodynamics of Party Popper Streamers" or "Vibe-Optimized Subwoofer Enclosure"
